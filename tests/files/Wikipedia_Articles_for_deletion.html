<p><b>Articles for deletion</b> (<b>AfD</b>) is where <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Wikipedians" title="Wikipedia:Wikipedians">Wikipedians</a> discuss whether an article should be deleted. Articles listed are normally discussed for at least seven days, after which the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_process" title="Wikipedia:Deletion process">deletion process</a> proceeds based on community <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>. Common outcomes are that the article is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD">kept</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD-M" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD-M">merged</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD-R" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD-R">redirected</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD-I" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD-I">incubated</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Moving_a_page" title="Wikipedia:Moving a page">renamed/moved</a> to another title, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Userfication" title="Wikipedia:Userfication">userfied</a> to a user subpage, or deleted per the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_policy" title="Wikipedia:Deletion policy">deletion policy</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">Disambiguation pages</a> are also nominated for deletion at AfD.
</p><p>This page explains what you should consider <a href="#Before_nominating:_checks_and_alternatives">before nominating</a>, the steps for nominating, and how to discuss an AfD. It also links to the lists of current debates, and two companion processes to AfD: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_deletion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Speedy deletion">speedy deletion</a> has a clearly defined set of criteria such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vandalism" title="Wikipedia:Vandalism">vandalism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Patent_nonsense" title="Wikipedia:Patent nonsense">patent nonsense</a>, whereas <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Proposed_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Proposed deletion">proposed deletion</a> is used to suggest deletions that no editor would contest.
</p><p><b>If you want to nominate an article</b>, the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_policy" title="Wikipedia:Deletion policy">Wikipedia deletion policy</a> explains the criteria for deletion, and may help you understand when an article should be nominated for deletion. The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Guide_to_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Guide to deletion">guide to deletion</a> explains the deletion process. If an article meets the criteria for deletion and you understand the process, consult <a href="#How_to_nominate_a_single_page_for_deletion">the instructions below</a>. If you are <b>unsure</b> whether a page should be nominated for deletion, or if you need more help, try <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia_talk:Articles_for_deletion" title="Wikipedia talk:Articles for deletion">this talk page</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Help_desk" title="Wikipedia:Help desk">Wikipedia's help desk</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contributing_to_AfD_discussions">Contributing to AfD discussions</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wikietiquette">Wikietiquette</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="WP:AFDEQ"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:AFDEQ&amp;redirect=no">WP:AFDEQ</a></span></li></ul></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Guide_to_deletion#General_advice" title="Wikipedia:Guide to deletion">Wikipedia:Guide to deletion &#160;General advice</a></div>
<ul><li>Users participating in AfD discussions are expected to be familiar with the policy of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Civility" title="Wikipedia:Civility">civility</a> and the guidelines <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Etiquette" title="Wikipedia:Etiquette">Wikietiquette</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_do_not_bite_the_newcomers" title="Wikipedia:Please do not bite the newcomers">"do not bite the newbies"</a>.
<ul><li>This also applies to the other deletion pages.</li></ul></li>
<li>AfDs are public, and are sometimes quoted in the popular press.<sup id="cite_ref-economist_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-economist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Please keep to public-facing levels of civility, just as you should for any edit you make to Wikipedia.</li>
<li>Avoid <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_personal_attacks" title="Wikipedia:No personal attacks">personal attacks</a> against people who disagree with you; avoid the use of sarcastic language and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Staying_cool_when_the_editing_gets_hot" title="Wikipedia:Staying cool when the editing gets hot">stay cool</a>.</li>
<li>Do not make unsourced negative comments about living people. These may be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GRAPEVINE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GRAPEVINE">removed by any editor</a>.</li>
<li>Remember that while AfD may look like a voting process, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Polling_is_not_a_substitute_for_discussion" title="Wikipedia:Polling is not a substitute for discussion">it does not operate like one</a>. Justification and evidence for a response carries far more weight than the response itself. Thus, you should not attempt to structure the AfD process like a vote:
<ul><li>Do not add tally boxes to the deletion page.</li>
<li>Do not reorder comments on the deletion page to group them by keep, delete, or other. Such reordering can disrupt the flow of discussion, polarize an issue, and emphasize vote count or word count.</li>
<li>Do not message editors about AfD nominations because they support your view on the topic. This can be seen as votestacking. See <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">Wikipedia:Canvassing</a> for guidelines. But if you are proposing deletion of an article, you can send a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Canvassing#Friendly_notices" title="Wikipedia:Canvassing">friendly notice</a> to those who contributed significantly to it and therefore might disagree with you.</li></ul></li>
<li>If a number of similar articles are to be nominated, it is best to make this a group nomination so that they can be considered collectively. This avoids excessive repetition which would otherwise tend to overload involved editors. However, group nominations that are too large or too loosely related may be split up or speedy-closed.</li>
<li>While there is no prohibition against moving an article while an AfD discussion is in progress, editors considering doing so should realize such a move can confuse the discussion greatly, can preempt a closing decision, can make the discussion difficult to track, and can lead to inconsistencies when using semi-automated closing <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Scripts" title="Wikipedia:Scripts">scripts</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_contribute">How to contribute</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="WP:AFDFORMAT"></span><span id="WP:DISCUSSAFD"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:AFDFORMAT&amp;redirect=no">WP:AFDFORMAT</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:DISCUSSAFD&amp;redirect=no">WP:DISCUSSAFD</a></span></li></ul></div>
<p>AfDs are a place for rational discussion of whether an article is able to meet Wikipedia's article guidelines and policies. Reasonable editors will often disagree, but valid arguments will be given more weight than unsupported statements. When an editor offers arguments or evidence that do not explain how the article meets/violates policy, they may only need a reminder to engage in constructive, on-topic discussion. But a pattern of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Arguments_to_avoid_in_deletion_discussions#Personal_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Arguments to avoid in deletion discussions">groundless opinion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Proof_by_assertion" title="Proof by assertion">proof by assertion</a>, and ignoring content guidelines may become disruptive. If a pattern of disruptive behavior persists after efforts are made to correct the situation through dialogue, please consider a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Dispute_resolution" title="Wikipedia:Dispute resolution">dispute resolution</a> process outside the current AfD.
</p><p>There are a number of practices that most Wikipedians use in AfD discussions:
</p>
<ul><li>When editors recommend a course of action, they usually do so in <b><a href="/wiki/MOS:BOLD" class="mw-redirect" title="MOS:BOLD">bold</a> text</b>, <i>e. g.</i>, "<b>Keep</b>", "<b>Delete</b>", "<b>Merge</b>", "<b>Redirect</b>", or other view. A number of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:LABS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:LABS">tools</a> which parse AfDs will only recognize bolded words.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Start comments or recommendations on a new bulleted line (that is, starting with <code>*</code>), and sign them by adding <code>~~~~</code> to the end. If you are responding to another editor, put your comment directly below theirs, making sure it is indented (using multiple <code>*</code>s).</li>
<li>If you want to expand on your own comments, add further text to your existing comments in preference to creating a new section. You can start a new paragraph indented the same as your bulleted paragraph by starting a line with a colon (<code>:</code>).</li>
<li>Please do not accompany comments with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Voting_templates" title="Wikipedia:Voting templates">label templates</a>.</li>
<li>Please disclose whether you have a vested interest in the article, per <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AVOIDCOI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AVOIDCOI">WP:AVOIDCOI</a>.</li>
<li>Please have a look at the article before making a recommendation. Do not base your recommendation solely on the information supplied by the nominator or other editors. To understand the situation, it may also help to look at the history of the article. However, please read the earlier comments and recommendations. They may contain relevant arguments and further useful information.</li></ul>
<p>When participating, please consider the following:
</p>
<ul><li>The debate is not a vote; please do not make recommendations on the course of action to be taken that are not sustained by arguments.</li>
<li>When making your case or responding to others, explain <i>how</i> the article meets/violates policy rather than merely stating that it meets/violates the policy.</li>
<li>Use of multiple accounts to reinforce your opinions is absolutely forbidden. Multiple recommendations by users shown to be using "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Sock_puppet" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Sock puppet">sock puppets</a>" (multiple accounts belonging to the same person) will be discounted and the user manipulating consensus with multiple accounts will likely be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BLOCK" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BLOCK">blocked indefinitely</a>.</li>
<li>You can explain your earlier recommendation in response to others but do not repeat a bolded recommendation on a new bulleted line.</li>
<li><span id="nom-already-implies"></span>Nomination already implies that the nominator recommends deletion (unless indicated otherwise), and nominators should refrain from repeating this recommendation on a separate bulleted line.</li>
<li>Do not make conflicting recommendations; if you change your mind, modify your original recommendation rather than adding a new one. The recommended way of doing this is to use strike-through by enclosing a retracted statement between <code>&lt;s&gt;</code> and <code>&lt;/s&gt;</code> after the <code>*</code>, as in <span class="nowrap">" <b><s>Delete</s> Keep</b>"</span>.</li>
<li>Unregistered or new users are welcome to contribute to the discussion, but their recommendations may be discounted if they seem to be made in bad faith (for example, if they misrepresent their reasons). Conversely, the opinions of logged in users whose accounts predate the article's AfD nomination may be given more weight when determining consensus.</li></ul>
<p>There are many good ways to advocate keeping, deleting or even redirecting an article. This includes:
</p>
<ul><li>Arguments commonly used to recommend deletion are: "unverifiable" (violates <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">WP:V</a>), "original research" (violates <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOR">WP:NOR</a>), and "non-notable" in cases where the subject does not meet their respective <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_notability_guidelines" title="Category:Wikipedia notability guidelines">notability criteria</a>. (In the cases of non-notable biographical articles, it is better to say "does not meet <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BIO" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BIO">WP:BIO</a>" to avoid insulting the subject.) Accusations of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:VAIN" title="Wikipedia:VAIN">vanity</a> and other motives should be avoided and is not in itself a reason for deletion. The argument "non-neutral point of view" (violates <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">WP:NPOV</a>) is often used, but often such articles can be salvaged, so this is not a very strong reason for deletion either.</li>
<li>If you wish for an article to be kept, you can directly improve the article to address the reasons for deletion given in the nomination. You can search out <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">reliable sources</a>, and refute the deletion arguments given using policy, guidelines, and examples from our <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GA">good</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FA">featured</a> articles. If you believe the article topic is valid and encyclopedic, and it lacks only references and other minor changes to survive, you may request help in the task by listing the article on the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_Rescue_Squadron_-_Rescue_list" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Article Rescue Squadron - Rescue list">rescue list</a> in accordance with instructions given at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RSL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RSL">WP:RSL</a>, and then adding the &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Rescue_list" title="Template:Rescue list">rescue list</a>&#125;&#125; template to the AfD discussion by posting <span style="white-space:nowrap;"><code>&#123;&#123;subst:rescue list&#125;&#125;</code></span> to the discussion thread. Please do <i>not</i> do this for articles which are likely to be eventually deleted on grounds other than simple incompleteness or poor writing (see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SNOW" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SNOW">WP:SNOW</a>).<div class="paragraphbreak" style="margin-top:0.5em"></div>If the reasons given in the deletion nomination are later addressed by editing, the nomination should be withdrawn by the nominator, and the deletion discussion will be closed by an admin. If the nominator fails to do it when you think it should have been done (people can be busy, so <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AGF" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AGF">assume good faith</a> on this point), leave a note on the nominator's talk page to draw their attention.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD">Alternatives to deletion</a> should be considered. If you think the article should be a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Disambiguation" title="Wikipedia:Disambiguation">disambiguation page</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:REDIRECT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:REDIRECT">redirect</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:MERGE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:MERGE">merger</a> to another article, then recommend "<b>Disambiguation</b>", "<b>Redirect</b>" or "<b>Merge</b>". Do not recommend deletion in such cases.</li></ul>
<p>You do not have to make a recommendation on every nomination; consider <i>not</i> participating if:
</p>
<ul><li>A nomination involves a topic with which you are unfamiliar.</li>
<li>You agree with the consensus that has already been formed.</li></ul>
<p>Please also see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability" title="Wikipedia:Notability">Wikipedia:Notability</a>.
</p>

<p>Prior to nominating article(s) for deletion, please be sure to:
</p>
<dl><dt>A. Read and understand these policies and guidelines</dt>
<dd></dd></dl>
<ol><li>The <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_policy" title="Wikipedia:Deletion policy">Wikipedia deletion policy</a>, which explains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DEL#REASON" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DEL">valid grounds for deletion</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD">alternatives to deletion</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DEL#PROCESSES" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DEL">various deletion processes</a></li>
<li>The main four guidelines and policies that inform deletion discussions: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability" title="Wikipedia:Notability">notability</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:N" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:N">WP:N</a>), <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verifiability</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">WP:V</a>), <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">reliable sources</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RS">WP:RS</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">what Wikipedia is not</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOT">WP:NOT</a>)</li>
<li>Subject-specific notability guidelines, which can be found at <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_notability_guidelines" title="Category:Wikipedia notability guidelines">Category:Wikipedia notability guidelines</a>, with further related essays at <a href="/wiki/Category:Wikipedia_notability" title="Category:Wikipedia notability">Category:Wikipedia notability</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion/Common_outcomes" title="Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Common outcomes">Common outcomes</a> may be checked to see if other articles on a specific topic tend to be kept or deleted after an AfD discussion</li></ol>
<dl><dt>B. Carry out these checks</dt>
<dd></dd></dl>
<ol><li>Confirm that the article does not meet the criteria for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_deletion" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Speedy deletion">speedy deletion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Proposed_deletion" title="Wikipedia:Proposed deletion">proposed deletion</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_keep" title="Wikipedia:Speedy keep">speedy keep</a>.</li>
<li>If there are <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">verifiability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:N" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:N">notability</a> or other sourcing concerns, take reasonable steps to search for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:IRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:IRS">reliable sources</a>. (See step D.)</li>
<li>Review the article's history to check for potential vandalism or poor editing.</li>
<li>Read the article's <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Talk_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Talk page">talk page</a> for previous nominations and/or that your objections haven't already been dealt with.</li>
<li>Check "<a href="/wiki/Help:What_links_here" title="Help:What links here">What links here</a>" in the article's sidebar, to see how the page is used and referenced within Wikipedia.</li>
<li>Check if there are <a href="/wiki/Help:Interlanguage_links" title="Help:Interlanguage links">interlanguage</a> links, also in the sidebar, which may lead to more developed and better-sourced articles. Likewise, search for native-language sources if the subject has a name in a non-<a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" title="Latin alphabet">Latin alphabet</a> (such as Japanese or Greek), which is often in the lede.</li></ol>
<dl><dt>C. Consider whether the article could be improved rather than deleted</dt></dl>
<ol><li>If the article can be fixed through <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ATD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ATD">normal editing</a>, then it is not a candidate for AfD.</li>
<li>If the article was recently created, please consider allowing the contributors more time to develop the article.</li>
<li>If an article has issues try first raising your concerns on the article's talk page, with the main contributors, or an associated WikiProject, and/or adding <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Template_messages/Cleanup" title="Wikipedia:Template messages/Cleanup">a cleanup tag</a>, such as <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Notability" title="Template:Notability">notability</a>}}</code>, <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Hoax" title="Template:Hoax">hoax</a>}}</code>, <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Original_research" title="Template:Original research">original research</a>}}</code>, or <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Advert" title="Template:Advert">advert</a>}}</code>; this ensures readers are aware of the problem and may act to remedy it.</li>
<li>If the topic is not important enough to merit an article on its own, consider <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Merge" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Merge">merging</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Redirect" title="Wikipedia:Redirect">redirecting</a> to an existing article. This should be done particularly if the topic name is a likely search term.</li></ol>
<dl><dt>D. Search for additional sources, if the main concern is notability</dt>
<dd></dd></dl>
<ol><li>The minimum search expected is a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.google.com/">normal Google search</a>, a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://books.google.com/">Google Books</a> search, a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://news.google.com">Google News</a> search, and a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://news.google.com/newspapers">Google News <i>archive</i></a> search; <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://scholar.google.com/">Google Scholar</a> is suggested for academic subjects.</li>
<li>If you find a <i>lack of sources</i>, you've completed basic due diligence before nominating. However, if a quick search does find sources, this does not always mean an AfD on a sourcing basis is unwarranted. If you spend more time examining the sources and determine that they are insufficient, e.g., because they only contain passing mention of the topic, then an AfD nomination may still be appropriate.</li>
<li>If you find that adequate sources <i>do appear to <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NEXIST" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NEXIST">exist</a></i>, the fact that they are not yet present in the article is not a proper basis for a nomination. Instead, you should consider citing the sources, using the advice in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:How_to_cite_sources" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:How to cite sources">Wikipedia:How to cite sources</a>, or at minimum apply an appropriate template to the page that flags the sourcing concern. Common templates include &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Unreferenced" title="Template:Unreferenced">unreferenced</a>&#125;&#125;, &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Refimprove" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:Refimprove">refimprove</a>&#125;&#125;, &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Third-party" title="Template:Third-party">third-party</a>&#125;&#125;, &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:Primary_sources" title="Template:Primary sources">primary sources</a>&#125;&#125; and &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:One_source" title="Template:One source">one source</a>&#125;&#125;. For a more complete list see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CTT" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CTT">WP:CTT</a>.</li></ol>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_nominate_multiple_related_pages_for_deletion">How to nominate <i>multiple related pages</i> for deletion</span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="WP:MULTIAFD"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:MULTIAFD&amp;redirect=no">WP:MULTIAFD</a></span></li></ul></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BUNDLE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BUNDLE">WP:BUNDLE</a></div>
<p>Sometimes you will find a number of related articles, all of which you feel should be deleted together. To make it easier for those participating in the discussion, it may be helpful to bundle all of them together into a single nomination. However, for group nominations, it is often a good idea to only list one article at afd and see how it goes, before listing an entire group.
</p><p>Examples of articles which may be bundled into a single nomination:
</p>
<ul><li>A group of articles with identical content but with slightly different titles.</li>
<li>A group of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:HOAX" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:HOAX">hoax articles</a> by the same editor.</li>
<li>A group of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ARTSPAM" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ARTSPAM">spam articles</a> by the same editor.</li>
<li>A series of articles on nearly identical manufactured products.</li></ul>
<p>An article with a fair or better chance of standing on its own merits should not be bundled nominate it separately. For the avoidance of doubt, bundling should not be used to form consensus around policy decisions such as "should Wikipedia include this type of article". Bundling AfDs should be used only for clear-cut deletion discussions based on existing policy. <b>If you're unsure, don't bundle it.</b>
</p><p>For the sake of clarity, debates should be bundled <i>only</i> at the start or near the start of the debate, ideally before any substantive discussion, but may be acceptable following one or two other editors' comments, particularly (but not only) where those comments are "per nom", by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:SPA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:SPA">single purpose accounts</a>, the article creator, or were clearly in bad faith.
</p><p><b>To bundle articles for deletion:</b>
</p>

</p>

</p>

</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_nominating:_Notify_interested_projects_and_editors">After nominating: Notify interested projects and editors</span></h3>
<p>While it is sufficient to list an article for discussion at AfD <i>(see above)</i>, nominators and others sometimes want to attract more attention from and participation by informed editors. All such efforts must comply with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CANVASS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CANVASS">Wikipedia's guideline against biased canvassing</a>.
</p><p>To encourage participation by less experienced editors, please avoid Wikipedia-specific abbreviations in the messages you leave about the discussion, link to any relevant policies or guidelines, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:N" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:N">notability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:V" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:V">verifiability</a> or a specific section of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not</a>, e.g., <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTDIR" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTDIR">Wikipedia is not a directory</a>, and please provide a link to the AfD discussion page itself.
</p>
<dl><dt>Deletion sorting</dt></dl>
<p>Once listed, deletion discussions can, optionally, also be transcluded into an appropriate <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Deletion_sorting" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Deletion sorting">deletion sorting</a> category, such as the ones for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Deletion_sorting/Actors_and_actresses" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Deletion sorting/Actors and actresses">actors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Deletion_sorting/Music" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Deletion sorting/Music">music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Deletion_sorting/Academics_and_educators" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Deletion sorting/Academics and educators">academics</a>, or for specific countries. Since many people watch deletion sorting pages for subject areas that particularly interest them, including your recent AfD listing on one of these pages helps attract people familiar with a particular topic area. Please see the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Deletion_sorting/Compact" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Deletion sorting/Compact">complete list of categories</a>.
<span id="wikiProjects"></span>
</p>
<dl><dt>Notifying related WikiProjects</dt></dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProjects</a> are groups of editors that are interested in a particular subject or type of editing. If the article is within the scope of one or more WikiProjects, they may welcome a brief, neutral note on their project's talk page(s) about the AfD.
</p><p>Tagging the nominated article's talk page with a relevant Wikiproject's banner will result in the article being listed in that project's <i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AALERTS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AALERTS">Article Alerts</a></i> automatically if they <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_alerts/Subscription_list" title="Wikipedia:Article alerts/Subscription list">subscribe to the system</a>. For instance, tagging an article with &#123;&#123;<a href="/wiki/Template:WikiProject_Physics" title="Template:WikiProject Physics">WikiProject Physics</a>&#125;&#125; will list the discussion in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Physics/Article_alerts" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Physics/Article alerts">Wikipedia:WikiProject Physics/Article alerts</a>.
</p><p><span id="substantial"></span>
</p>
<dl><dt>Notifying substantial contributors to the article</dt></dl>
<p>While not required, it is generally considered courteous to notify the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Assume_good_faith" title="Wikipedia:Assume good faith">good-faith</a> creator and any main contributors of the articles that you are nominating for deletion. One should not notify <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Bot_policy" title="Wikipedia:Bot policy">bot accounts</a>, people who have made only insignificant 'minor' edits, or people who have never edited the article. To find the main contributors, look in the <a href="/wiki/Help:Page_history" title="Help:Page history">page history</a> or <a href="/wiki/Help:Talk_page" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:Talk page">talk page</a> of the article and/or use the <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/xtools-articleinfo/">Page History tool</a> or <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://vs.aka-online.de/wppagehiststat/">Wikipedia Page History Statistics</a>. Use: <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Substitution" title="Wikipedia:Substitution">subst</a>:<a href="/wiki/Template:Afd_notice" title="Template:Afd notice">Afd notice</a>&#124;<var>article name</var>&#124;<var>AfD discussion title</var>}} ~~~~</code>
</p><p>At this point, you've done all you need to do as nominator. Sometime after seven days have passed, someone will either close the discussion or, where needed, "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RELIST" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RELIST">relist</a>" it for another seven days of discussion. (The "someone" <i>must not</i> be you, the nominator. However, if you want to see how it's done, refer to the next section.)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Withdrawing_a_nomination">Withdrawing a nomination</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="#Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_(nominator_withdrawal)"> &#160;Procedure for non-administrator close (nominator withdrawal)</a></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="WP:WDAFD"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcut</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WDAFD&amp;redirect=no">WP:WDAFD</a></span></li></ul></div>
<p>If no one else has supported the deletion proposal and you change your mind about the nomination, you can withdraw it. This might be because the discussion has produced new information about the topic, or because you realise the nomination was a mistake. Withdrawing a nomination can save other editors' time by cutting short the discussion.
</p><p>To withdraw a nomination, add a note saying "<b>Withdrawn by nominator</b>" immediately below your nomination statement at the top of the discussion, give a brief explanation, and sign it.
</p><p>If no one has supported deletion of the article you may close the discussion yourself as a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_keep" title="Wikipedia:Speedy keep">WP:Speedy keep</a>, or you may leave it for someone else to close the discussion.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_an_AfD_discussion_is_closed">How an AfD discussion is closed</span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main pages: <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_process#Articles_for_deletion_page" title="Wikipedia:Deletion process">Wikipedia:Deletion process &#160;Articles for deletion page</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion/Administrator_instructions" title="Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Administrator instructions">Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Administrator instructions</a></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r911143475"/><div class="module-shortcutanchordiv"><span id="WP:CLOSEAFD"></span><span id="WP:NotEarly"></span></div><div class="module-shortcutboxplain plainlist noprint" role="note"><div class="module-shortcutlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Shortcut" title="Wikipedia:Shortcut">Shortcuts</a></div><ul><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:CLOSEAFD&amp;redirect=no">WP:CLOSEAFD</a></span></li><li><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:NotEarly&amp;redirect=no">WP:NotEarly</a></span></li></ul></div>
<ul><li>A deletion discussion should normally be allowed to run for seven full days (168 hours).</li>
<li>Consensus is not based on a tally of votes, but on reasonable, logical, policy-based arguments.</li>
<li>The AfD nominator can withdraw the nomination <i>and</i> close a discussion as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_keep" title="Wikipedia:Speedy keep">speedy keep</a> reason #1, if <i>all</i> other viewpoints expressed were for <b>Keep</b> <i>and</i> doing so does not short-circuit an ongoing discussion. For how to perform this, see below, subsection <a href="#Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_(nominator_withdrawal)">Procedure for non-administrator close (nominator withdrawal)</a></li>
<li>An <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Administrators" title="Wikipedia:Administrators">admin</a> who is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:UNINVOLVED" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:UNINVOLVED">uninvolved</a> and has not participated in the deletion discussion will assess the discussion for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Consensus" title="Wikipedia:Consensus">consensus</a>. For how to perform this, see <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AFD/AI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AFD/AI">WP:AFD/AI</a>.</li>
<li>An editor in good standing who is not an administrator, and is also uninvolved, may close AfDs in certain circumstances; appropriate closures that non-admins may make are detailed at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-admin_closure#Appropriate_closures" title="Wikipedia:Non-admin closure">Wikipedia:Non-admin closure &#160;Appropriate closures</a>. For how to perform this, see below, subsection <a href="#Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_(other)">Procedure for non-administrator close (other)</a></li>
<li>If consensus seems unclear the outcome can be listed as <b>No consensus</b> (with no effect on the article's status) or the discussion may be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RELIST" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RELIST">relisted</a> for further discussion.</li>
<li>A discussion can be closed sooner than seven days if <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_process#Early_closure" title="Wikipedia:Deletion process">any of certain special conditions</a> applies.</li>
<li>Questions or concerns about a closure should first be asked on the <a href="/wiki/Help:Using_talk_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Help:Using talk pages">talk page</a> of the editor who closed the discussion. If that does not resolve the concerns, the closure can be appealed at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Deletion_review" title="Wikipedia:Deletion review">Wikipedia:Deletion review</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span id="Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_.28nominator_withdrawal.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_(nominator_withdrawal)">Procedure for non-administrator close (nominator withdrawal)</span></h3>
<p>As mentioned <a href="#How_an_AfD_discussion_is_closed">above</a>, the AfD nominator can withdraw the nomination <i>and</i> close a discussion as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Speedy_keep" title="Wikipedia:Speedy keep">speedy keep</a> reason #1, if <i>all</i> other viewpoints expressed were for <b>Keep</b> <i>and</i> doing so does not short-circuit an ongoing discussion.
</p><p>This procedure involves performing edits to three pages, as follows:
</p>
<ul><li>On the deletion discussion page
<ul><li>Remove the <code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:AfDh" title="Template:AfDh">AfDh</a>}}</code>/<code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:AfDb" title="Template:AfDb">AfDb</a>}}</code>/<code>{{<a href="/wiki/Template:Closing" title="Template:Closing">Closing</a>}}</code> tags from the page, if they were placed beforehand.</li>

<li>Insert at the bottom of the page: <span style="color: green;">{{subst:Afd bottom}}</span></li>

<ul><li>Find the article page

<h3><span id="Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_.28other.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_(other)">Procedure for non-administrator close (other)</span></h3>
<p>As mentioned <a href="#How_an_AfD_discussion_is_closed">above</a>, an editor in good standing who is not an administrator, and is also uninvolved, may close AfDs in certain circumstances; appropriate closures that non-admins may make are detailed at <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Non-admin_closure#Appropriate_closures" title="Wikipedia:Non-admin closure">Wikipedia:Non-admin closure &#160;Appropriate closures</a>.
</p><p>For a result of "keep", this procedure differs from the <a href="#Procedure_for_non-administrator_close_(nominator_withdrawal)">Procedure for non-administrator close (nominator withdrawal)</a> above, only in the reasons to be listed in the templates and the comments to be annotated in the edit summaries. Follow those instructions, replacing references to "keep (nomination withdrawn)" with the relevant reason.
</p><p>For any other appropriate result, the procedure is basically the same, with the differences listed in <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Articles_for_deletion/Administrator_instructions#Carrying_out_the_AfD_close" title="Wikipedia:Articles for deletion/Administrator instructions">WP:Articles for deletion/Administrator instructions#Carrying out the AfD close</a>.
</p>
